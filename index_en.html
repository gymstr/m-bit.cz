01.

ASM80.com

2013 — ?

The ASM80.com project combines my two hobbies into one. It is an online development environment, written entirely in JavaScript. The environment includes, besides an editor and Assembler, and several 8-bit emulators processors.

ASM80.com can compile programs for the Intel 8080 and 8008 processors, MOS 6502, Zilog Z80, Motorola 6800 and 6809, WDC 65C816, CDP 1802 and others...

The whole environment runs fully in the browser, no need to install anything.

I wrote three books that were published in Edition CZ.NIC.

The first book, Shafts, Volts, Monochips, presents an introduction to microelectronics and digital technology. I carry the reader from the very basics (voltage, current, resistance, Ohm's law) through basic electronic components and a range of TTL digital circuits. (74xx) to specialized components such as sensors, semiconductor memories and single-chip computers.

In the second book, Ports, Bytes, Osmibits I explain, using the example of three detailed designs, the basic principles of osmibit computers, their practical design and wiring. OMEN designs use the legendary eight-bit Intel 8085, MOS 65C02 and Motorola 6809 processors. The book describes not only their circuitry, but also discusses their programming in machine code (assembler).

In 2020, a third book, Data, Chips, Processors, was published, covering the basics of VHDL for designing custom electronic components (FPGAs) and its practical applications.

03.

OMEN

2018 — ?

For my books I have prepared several constructions of eight-bit computers. I called these designs OMEN - Osmibit Microcomputers for Electronic Enthusiasts.

These are very simple designs, containing mostly just processor, memory, and serial and parallel ports. Yet with these computers can be worked with and are functionally on par with the 8-bit machines of the early 1980s. The advantage is that they are open designs with a free license, so they can be freely modified.

EduShield

2017 — 2019

With Štěpán Bechynský we started a course on the basics of working with Arduino, but it turned out that we lacked a suitable teaching kit. So we designed and in cooperation with the CZ.NIC association we designed and produced a EduShield, which contains a set of of peripherals designed to demonstrate several interesting circuitry to cover a wide range of areas encountered when working with Arduino, from working with sensors to interrupts and advanced communications.
